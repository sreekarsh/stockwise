Account Basics

Create Account

Click Login in the top-right corner of any page, then select Create Account. Fill in your username, email address, and password. Password must be at least 8 characters with at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character (e.g. !@#$%). Once submitted, your account is created and you are logged in automatically. A default avatar is generated for you — you can customise it later in the Avatar section.

Use a unique password that you don’t reuse on other sites. StockWise stores passwords using bcrypt hashing — we never see your plain-text password.
Login

Click Login in the top-right corner. Enter your email and password, then click Sign In. You can optionally check Remember Me to stay logged in across browser sessions. If you already have an active session, the login button will show your username instead — click it to access your profile or log out.

If you see a “Invalid credentials” error, double-check your email and password. Passwords are case-sensitive. If you’ve forgotten your password, use the Forgot Password link on the login form.
Reset Password

On the login form, click Forgot Password. Enter the email address associated with your account. A password reset token will be generated and displayed on screen (in the current build, the token is shown directly — copy it). Then enter your new password (must meet the same strength requirements as registration) and the reset token, then click Reset Password. If the token matches, your password is updated immediately and you can log in with the new password.

Reset tokens are single-use and expire after 15 minutes. If the token expires, request a new one. Never share your reset token with anyone.
After resetting, log out of all other devices by going to Profile → Security and clicking Revoke All Sessions.

Signals

Core

StockWise generates BUY / SELL / HOLD signals using a Temporal Fusion Transformer (TFT) ensemble — a deep learning architecture designed for time-series forecasting. The model ingests multiple market data streams (price, volume, volatility, order book imbalance, social sentiment) and outputs probabilistic predictions with interpretable explanations. Signals update periodically as new training data becomes available.

Understanding a Signal Card
Signal Type BUY (green), SELL (red), or HOLD (yellow). Based on the model’s highest-probability prediction for the next time window.
Confidence Score A 0–100% value indicating how strongly the model favours this signal. Higher confidence means greater agreement across the ensemble.
Entry Price The recommended price level to enter the trade. For BUY signals this is near the current ask; for SELL signals near the current bid.
Target Price The projected price level where the model suggests taking profit. Adjusts automatically when you change the risk-reward ratio.
Stop-Loss An exit price to limit downside risk. Calculated based on recent support/resistance levels and volatility. Moves with risk-reward adjustments.
Risk-Reward Slider Drag to adjust your desired reward ratio (e.g. 1:2, 1:3). The target and stop prices update dynamically to reflect the new ratio.
SHAP Explain Click to open an interactive bar chart showing which market factors (momentum, RSI, volume, volatility, spread, etc.) influenced the signal — and whether each factor pushed toward buy or sell. Built on SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) from coalitional game theory.
Signal Table A sortable, filterable table listing all generated signals. Sort by confidence, expected return %, or timestamp. Filter by signal type (BUY/SELL/HOLD) or search by coin name.
How the ML Model Works
  • The TFT ensemble combines 5 independently trained TFT models with different random seeds and validation splits.
  • Each model learns temporal dependencies — how price action, volume, and order book data interact over time to produce future movements.
  • The ensemble reduces variance: final predictions are the probability-weighted average across all models.
  • SHAP values decompose each prediction into additive contributions from every input feature, showing exactly why the model said BUY vs SELL.
  • The model is re-trained on a rolling window of data. The Analyzer page shows training status, fold-level backtest metrics (win rate, Sharpe ratio, profit factor), and feature importance rankings.
High confidence BUY/SELL signals with strong SHAP alignment on momentum + volume factors historically show the best backtest performance. Always verify with your own analysis.

CoinDCX API Key Setup

Why connect CoinDCX?

Linking your CoinDCX account syncs your real portfolio holdings, balances, and trade history into StockWise. Keys are encrypted with AES-256 before storage — StockWise never has access to your funds or withdrawal capabilities.

Security: Grant only Read-only permissions. Never share your secret key.
Step-by-Step Guide
1

Log in to CoinDCX

Go to coindcx.com and navigate to Settings → API Keys from your profile menu.

2

Create a New API Key

Click Generate API Key and label it StockWise for easy identification.

3

Set Permissions

Select Read-only only. StockWise reads balances and trade history — it never trades or withdraws. Uncheck all write/withdrawal permissions.

Granting write permissions is unnecessary and exposes you to risk. Read-only is all you need.
4

Copy Your Credentials

You’ll receive two values:

  • API Key — your public identifier (long alphanumeric string)
  • API Secret — keep this private (shown only once)
Save both to a password manager before closing the popup. The secret will not be shown again.
5

Add Keys to StockWise

Go to Profile → API Keys. Paste your API Key and Secret, then click Save Keys. StockWise encrypts and stores them securely.

6

Test the Connection

Click Sync Portfolio on the Portfolio page. A green Connected status confirms everything works. If it fails, verify your key permissions and try again from Profile → API Keys.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is StockWise free? +
Yes. Live tracking, ML signals, community chat, portfolio analysis, and bot trading with $10,000 demo funds are all free. No credit card required.
How do I create an account? +
Click Login in the top-right corner of any page, then select Create Account. Enter a username, your email, and a password (8+ characters with uppercase, lowercase, number, and special character). Submit and you’re logged in automatically with a default avatar.
How do I reset my password? +
On the login form, click Forgot Password. Enter your email — a reset token is displayed on screen. Copy it, then enter your new password (same strength rules) and the token, then click Reset Password. Tokens are single-use and expire after 15 minutes.
Do I need a CoinDCX account? +
No. Almost all features work without one. CoinDCX is only needed to sync real portfolio holdings automatically. You can also track holdings manually on the Portfolio page.
Are the ML signals financial advice? +
No. Signals are generated by ML models for educational and informational purposes only. Always do your own research before making investment decisions.
How accurate are the ML signals? +
Accuracy varies by market conditions. The Analyzer page shows historical backtest metrics (win rate, Sharpe ratio, profit factor). Past performance does not guarantee future results.
Is my CoinDCX API key safe? +
Yes. Keys are encrypted with AES-256 before storage. StockWise only uses read-only endpoints. We never have the ability to trade or withdraw on your behalf.
What is the Bot Trading demo account? +
Every user gets a simulated $10,000 portfolio with real market data. Place trades, deploy automated bots, and track P&L — all risk-free. Reset your practice account anytime.
Where does market data come from? +
Cryptocurrency data is from CoinGecko and Binance WebSocket. Stock data is from Finnhub and Yahoo Finance. Data refreshes every 30 seconds.
Where can I get help? +
The Community section has live chat channels. For technical issues, check the project README or open a GitHub issue.
How do I set a price alert? +
On the Live Tracker page, click the bell icon next to any asset. Enter your target price and choose Above or Below. You’ll receive a browser notification when the price crosses your threshold. Manage all alerts from the bell icon in the top bar.
How does the watchlist work? +
Click the star icon on any asset in the Live Tracker to add it to your personal watchlist. Use the Watchlist filter tab to view only starred items. Your watchlist is saved to your account and syncs across sessions.
Can I export my portfolio data? +
Yes. On the Portfolio page, click the Export CSV button to download all your holdings, including quantities, entry prices, current values, and P&L. You can also export your trade history from the Bot Trading section.
What are SHAP explanations? +
SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) is a game-theoretic method that explains how each market factor contributed to an ML signal. On the Signals page, click SHAP Explain on any signal card to see a bar chart showing which factors (momentum, RSI, volume, volatility, etc.) pushed the prediction toward buy or sell, and by how much.
How do I reset my bot trading account? +
Go to the Bot Trading page and click Reset Account in the settings panel. This resets your virtual balance back to $10,000 and clears all open positions and trade history. Use this to start fresh with a new strategy.
What are the automated trading bots? +
Bot Trading offers three pre-built strategies: RSI Momentum (buys when RSI oversold, sells when overbought), MACD Crossover (trades on MACD line crossovers), and Grid Scalper (places multiple limit orders in a range). Deploy them on any asset with configurable parameters and let them trade autonomously in the demo environment.
Can I change my display currency? +
Yes. Go to Profile → Preferences and select your preferred currency from USD, INR, EUR, or GBP. The setting applies across all pages including Live Tracker, Portfolio, and Signals.
What happens if I delete my account? +
Deleting your account removes all personal data: your profile, portfolio holdings, watchlist, alerts, avatar, and bot trading history. This action is permanent and cannot be undone. To delete, go to Profile → Settings → Delete Account.
How is the Fear & Greed Index calculated? +
The Fear & Greed Index in the Community section aggregates signals from multiple sources: market momentum, trading volume, social media sentiment, surveys, and market dominance. It ranges from 0 (Extreme Fear) to 100 (Extreme Greed) and updates every hour as a quick pulse on market sentiment.
